Taj Mahal

Located in Agra, India. It was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal with construction starting in 1632 AD and completed in 1648 AD.

Greate Wall of China

Located at China Border. The wall is the longest man made structure in the world, with a total length of about 13170.7 mi or 21196.18 km.

Petra

Located in Jordan. Petra is half-built, half-carved into the rock, and is surrounded by mountains riddled with passages and gorges.

Colloseum

The Colosseum in Rome, Italy, is a large amphitheater that hosted events like gladiatorial games. The Colosseum, also named the Flavian Amphitheater, is a large amphitheater in Rome.

chichén itzá

Chichén Itzá, ruined ancient Maya city occupying an area of 4 square miles (10 square km) in south-central Yucatán state, Mexico.

Machu Picchu

Located in Peru. More than 7,000 feet above sea level in the Andes Mountains, Machu Picchu is the most visited tourist destination in Peru. A symbol of the Incan Empire and built around 1450AD

Christ the Redeemer

The statue of Christ the Redeemer was completed in 1931 and stands 98 feet (30 metres) tall, its horizontally outstretched arms spanning 92 feet (28 metres). The statue has become emblematic of both the city of Rio de Janeiro and the whole nation of Brazil.
